About This Map

GLOBE Observer Data: Real citizen science observations from the Global Learning and Observations to Benefit the Environment (GLOBE) Program. Community members worldwide report mosquito breeding sites and larvae counts.

NASA Satellite Integration: Environmental conditions overlay based on the same methodology used by NASA researchers Assaf Anyamba (Rift Valley Fever tracking) and Antar Jutla (cholera prediction). This approach has prevented disease outbreaks by identifying favorable mosquito breeding conditions weeks in advance.

Key Success: In 2016, NASA satellite data detected wetter-than-usual conditions in Kenya during El NiÃ±o. Early warnings enabled mass livestock vaccination, preventing a Rift Valley Fever outbreak despite large numbers of disease-carrying mosquitoes. No human or animal outbreaks were reported.

Know Your Vectors

Mosquito Species in Africa

Understanding which mosquitoes carry which diseases is crucial for targeted prevention.

Anopheles

Critical Risk

Primary malaria vector in Africa. Female Anopheles mosquitoes transmit Plasmodium parasites responsible for over 200 million malaria cases annually.

Active Time:
Dusk to dawn (nocturnal)
Breeding Sites:
Clean, sunlit water pools
Diseases:
Malaria, Lymphatic filariasis
Peak Season:
After heavy rainfall
Prevention: Bed nets, indoor spraying, drain standing water

Aedes aegypti

High Risk

Urban specialist mosquito. Recognized by white markings on legs. Transmits dengue, chikungunya, Zika, and yellow fever. Thrives in urban environments.

Active Time:
Daytime (dawn & dusk peaks)
Breeding Sites:
Containers, tires, flower pots
Diseases:
Dengue, Zika, Chikungunya, Yellow Fever
Peak Season:
Warm, humid conditions
Prevention: Remove containers, cover water storage, use repellent

Culex

Moderate Risk

Common brown mosquito. Transmits West Nile virus and Rift Valley fever. Breeds in polluted water. Key vector during flooding events.

Active Time:
Nighttime (nocturnal)
Breeding Sites:
Polluted water, sewage, flooded areas
Diseases:
West Nile, Rift Valley Fever, Filariasis
Peak Season:
Post-flooding, wet seasons
Prevention: Improve sanitation, drain flooded areas, window screens
